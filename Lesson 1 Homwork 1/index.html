The Simpsons are the main characters of the series "The Simpsons". The family first appeared on The Tracey Ullman Show in the comedy short Good Night. The family has hundreds of relatives, including Mr. Burns, Patty, and Selma.

homer simpson

Homer J. Simpson (born May 12, 1956) is the main character of the series (or series) "The Simpsons". He is the husband of Marge Simpson and the father of Bart, Lisa and Maggie Simpson. Homer is overweight (said to weigh ~240 pounds), lazy, and often ignorant of the world around him. Although Homer has many flaws, he has shown great care, love, and even bravery to those he cares about, and sometimes even to those he dislikes. He also performs the role of the main character of the movie "Simpsons".

Bart Simpson

Bartholomew "Bart" Jojo Simpson (born April 1 or February 23) is the mischievous, rebellious, misunderstood, destructive, and "potentially dangerous" eldest child of the Simpson family on The Simpsons. He is the only son of Homer and Marge Simpson and the older brother of Lisa and Maggie. He was also nicknamed "Cosmo" after the discovery of the comet in Barth's Comet. Bart has also been on the cover of numerous comics such as Critical Hit, Simpsons Treasure Trove #11, and Winter Wingding. Bart also has a 100-issue comic book series called The Simpson Comics Presents Bart Simpson. Matt Groening and his older brother Mark Groening. Even at a young age, Bart accomplished many feats. He won awards for his web series Bad Dad, discovered a new comet named after him, and performed in a successful band with Milhouse Van Houten, Nelson Muntz, and Ralph Wiggum.

Lisa Simpson

Lisa Marie Simpson (born May 9) is the eldest daughter and middle child of the Simpson family and one of the two tri-tagonists (along with Marge) of The Simpsons. In Homer and Lisa Crosswords, she is also known as Lisa Bouvier. She was named after a train called the Lil' Lisa on her parents' first anniversary. This is a charismatic 8-year-old girl who exceeds the standard achievements of the level of intelligence of children of her age. To everyone's surprise, she is also the moral center of her family. Lisa's upbringing lacks the parental involvement of Homer and Marge, leading to hobbies such as playing the saxophone and guitar, riding and caring for horses, and an interest in advanced studies. At school, Lisa's popularity is affected by those who see her as a nerd who doesn't keep up, leaving her with few friends. She is focused on her goals and eager to reach her potential, and at the age of eight, she is already a member of Mensa with an IQ of 159.
